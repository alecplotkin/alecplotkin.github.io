I'm a graduating PhD student in **Bioinformatics and Computational Biology** at **UNC-Chapel Hill**, advised by [Natalie Stanley](https://compcylab.squarespace.com/) and [Justin Milner](https://unclineberger.org/milnerlab/). I develop data-driven, biologically grounded computational models to understand T cell fate and immune responses.

My PhD work focuses on optimal transport (OT) fate mapping single-cell trajectories using time course data, specifically CD8 T cells during acute infection. I am interested in extending this work into generative modeling (e.g. using neural ODEs) for perturbation prediction, as well as pivoting to different biological contexts.

Prior to starting a PhD, I worked as a Research Assistant at Boston Children's Hospital's [Precision Vaccines Program](https://research.childrenshospital.org/research-units/pediatrics-research/precision-vaccines-program), where I researched neonatal immune ontogeny and vaccine responses. I received my B.S.Eng. in Chemical Engineering from Northeastern University.

I am currently looking for work in the Boston, MA area.
