I'm a graduating PhD student in Bioinformatics and Computational Biology at UNC-Chapel Hill (looking for work!). I'm interested in developing data-driven, biologically feasible computational models and applying them to make meaningful biological discoveries. I am co-mentored by Natalie Stanley (CompCy Lab) and Justin Milner.

My PhD work focused on using optimal transport to model single-cell trajectories from timecourse data, specifically CD8 T cells during acute infection. I am interested in extending this work into generative modeling (e.g. using neural ODEs) for perturbation prediction, as well as pivoting to different biological contexts. However, I am open to other projects as well!

Prior to starting a PhD, I worked as a Research Assistant at Boston Children's Hospital's Precision Vaccines Program, and received my B.S.Eng. in Chemical Engineering from Northeastern University.
